Information is Beautiful

Case Study : Coronavirus Riskiest Activities

In this blog post I will be analysing a graph/piece of visual data from the site, Information is beautiful. The case study that I will be looking at is titled Coronavirus Riskiest Activities , I will be looking at a graph that shows different activities that people can do or used to do and whether or not those activities are considered high, medium or low risk The data used for this graph is according to the site is information that was collected from different professionals,this is inclusive of epidemiologists and health workers.

Representation

In this graph, the activities are represented by different coloured and different sized circles. Low risk activities are represented by a yellow colour, the smaller less saturated circles represent activities with the lowest risk; while the more saturated ones represent activities that are more riskier than the ones with less saturation.

An important thing to note is the fact that that they do mention the conditions of keeping those low risk activities low risk, this is important because it shows that they are not trying to decieve or mislead people
